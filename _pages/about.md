I am a Mechatronic Systems Engineering student at Simon Fraser University, graduating in April 2026. My focus lies in embedded systems, electronics, and firmware development. From PCB design and hardware bring-up to real-time software and system validation. I enjoy working across the full stack of an electrical system, and am drawn to roles where hardware and software meet in a tangible, physical product.

Most recently, I worked as an Embedded Systems Engineer at Dometic, where I developed firmware for real-time embedded systems, built a HIL testing framework using CAN message spoofing to validate firmware under realistic conditions, and performed root-cause analysis on hardware failures down to the component level. Prior to that, I worked as a Service Engineer at Ballard Power Systems, where I developed maintenance procedures and documentation standards for hydrogen fuel cell components.

I have experience taking projects from requirements through to validated prototypes, and have held leadership roles in teams ranging from 4 to 20 members.
