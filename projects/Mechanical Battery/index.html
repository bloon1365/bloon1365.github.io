Mechanical Battery

A mechanical energy storage device made for a university project. Team Project

Project Overview

In a university course my team was tasked with developing a mechanical energy storage system aimed at supplementing intermittent energy sources like solar and wind.

Concept Exploration and Selection

A variety of mechanical energy storage systems were brainstormed and a selection matrix was used to find the system that best fit the problem.

  • Potential Concepts:
    - Pumped Hydro
    - Flywheel
    - Compression Spring System
    - Rubber Band Pulley

We scored all potential systems in these 5 categories, higher is better.

  • Evaluation Criteria:
    - Cost
    - Energy Stored
    - Efficiency
    - Complexity
    - Safety

The rubber band and pulley system was chosen at a score of 3.2, due its simplicity, ease of construction, and cost.

Conclusion and Key Takeaways

While the rubber band pulley system demonstrated the feasibility of mechanical energy storage, its low efficiency and limited energy output deem it impractical for widespread use. Nonetheless, the project provided valuable insights into alternative energy storage methods and served as a platform for applying engineering knowledge to real-world problems.
